## ----- RULES -----

### Game setup

Each player picks 3 dinosaurs.

Can be done randomly, by alternating picking one, or picking for your opponent.

### How to play

#### Phase A: The Attack

The attacker rolls a d6 and adds the result to their dinosaur’s Base Attack stat.

_Note: Even if the Base Attack is 0, the dice roll still counts as the total attack value._

#### Phase B: Evasion

The defender rolls a d4 and adds the result to their dinosaur’s Base Evasion stat.

**Miss:** If the Evasion total is HIGHER than the Attack total, the attack is dodged. The defender takes 0 damage and the turn ends.

**Hit:** If the Attack total is equal to or higher than the Evasion total, the attack connects.

#### Phase C: Mitigation (Damage Reduction)

If the attack hits, the defender rolls a d2 (1 or 2) to reduce the incoming damage.

Subtract the d2 result from the total Attack value.

_Note: Damage cannot go below 0._

#### Phase D: The "Spike" Counter-Attack

If the defender takes 1 or more damage and survives the hit, they may immediately trigger their Spike Attack.

**Fixed Damage:** Spikes deal a set amount of damage (no dice roll required).

_Note: If the attackers strike reduces the defending dino to 0 hitpoints, the spike attack do not trigger._

#### Phase E: Victory & Next Round

The battle continues until one dinosaur reaches 0 HP.

**The Next Selection:** Players must choose a dinosaur they have not used yet.

**The Winner's Penalty:** The player who won the previous round must choose their next dino first.

Both players must roll a new d6 to determine who takes the first turn in the new round.

_Note: the first player to get 2 victories has won the game._

### Example game

Example Game: **Stegosaurus** vs. **Velociraptor**

Phase 1: The Initiative

Both players roll to see who attacks first.

    Stegosaurus rolls a 4.

    Velociraptor rolls a 2.

    Result: Stegosaurus takes the first turn!

Phase 2: Stegosaurus Attacks

The Stegosaurus rolls attack.

    Attack Roll: Stegosaurus rolls its d6. It rolls a 5.

        Total Attack: 5(roll)+2(stat)=7

    Evasion Roll: Velociraptor tries to dodge using its d4. It rolls a 3.

        Total Evasion: 3(roll)+4(stat)=7

    Outcome: Since the Evasion (7) is not higher than the Attack (7), the hit connects!

Phase 3: Damage & Spikes

Now we see how much damage the Raptor actually takes.

    Defense Roll: Velociraptor rolls its d2 to negate damage. It rolls a 2.

        Total Defense: 2(roll)+0(stat)=2

    Damage Calculated: 7(Attack)−2(Defense)=5 damage.

        Velociraptor HP: 20→15

    Spike counter: Since the Velociraptor dose not have spikes, it dosen't deal any damage to the Stegosaurus.

Phase 4: Velociraptor Counter-Attacks

Now its the Velociraptoers turn

    Attack Roll: Velociraptor rolls its d6. It rolls a 2.

        Total Attack: 2(roll)+5(stat)=7

    Evasion Roll: Stegosaurus rolls its d4. It rolls a 1.

        Total Evasion: 1(roll)+0(stat)=1

    Defense Roll: Stegosaurus rolls its d2. It rolls a 1.

        Total Defense: 1(roll)+3(stat)=4

    Final Damage: 7(Attack)−4(Defense)=3 damage.

        Stegosaurus HP: 24→21

    Spike counter: Since the Stegosaurus have 1 in spike damage, it deals 1 damage to the Velociraptor

        Velociraptor HP: 15→14

Current Status:

    Stegosaurus: 21 HP

    Velociraptor: 14 HP

The battle continues until one dinosaur's hitpoints reach 0!
